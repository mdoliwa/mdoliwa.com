I started working as a Rails developer 4 years ago, after almost 10 years off-break from coding. I learned Ruby and Ruby on Rails by doing some tutorials and building small projects. When I got my first job offer I was a little scared as I was not familiar with large, existing codebases. All I knew were my little pet projects.

If you are in the same spot as I was a few years ago, or you’re just curious how others do this. Here is the list of the top 10 most popular Ruby on Rails repositories on GitHub.

## Top 10 most popular Rails repositories on GitHub

1. [Discourse](https://github.com/discourse/discourse) - discussion platform
2. [Mastodon](https://github.com/tootsuite/mastodon) - social network server based on ActivityPub
3. [GitLab](https://github.com/gitlabhq/gitlabhq) - end-to-end software development platform
4. [Forem](https://github.com/forem/forem) - software for building communities (powers [dev.to](https://dev.to))
5. [diaspora*](https://github.com/diaspora/diaspora) - privacy-aware, distributed, open source social network
6. [Postal](https://github.com/postalhq/postal) - complete and fully featured mail server for use by websites & web servers
7. [chatwoot](https://github.com/chatwoot/chatwoot) - Customer engagement suite, an open-source alternative to Intercom, Zendesk etc.
8. [OpenProject](https://github.com/opf/openproject) - web-based project management software
9. [Canvas LMS](https://github.com/instructure/canvas-lms) - 
Learning management system
10. [Peatio](https://github.com/peatio/peatio) - open-source crypto currency exchange
